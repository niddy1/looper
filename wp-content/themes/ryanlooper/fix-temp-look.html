SPLASH DECANT 30/05/2016

Sommelier Michael Madrigale to Leave Bou- lud Empire This Week

The current trend towards wine lists that have numerous verticals of producers has to stop. These programs are almost without exception ridiculous.

Listing all the vintages you can find from a producer to show your girth is excessive and feeds a ‘unicorn wine’ mentality. It highlights the pursuit of the most scarce, sommelebrity endorsed, “best” wines.

“ I know some guys in their 30’s who drive corvettes and go to high school parties too, getting lucky can happen.

The dirty secret of this style of wine list is that most of the wines listed are in various stagesof not enjoyable to drink.

If the goal is sheer size to win an award or to show how much ‘difficult to find’ wine you have, then fantastic! I know some guys in their 30’s who drive corve es and go to high school parties too..ge ing lucky can happen.

But if the aim is to highlight a producer in a thoughtful way, then the library of verticals wine list can’t be the direction.

What if on the wine list under that producer were only the vintages that are drinking well? Imagine a Clos Rougeard section with just the vintag- es that are open and ready to drink! This is an honorable way to high- light a producer.

I have to believe that if you buy something as a beverage director, put it on a list and then serve it to a table, it should drink well enough that you can look that customer in the eye when they leave. And shouldn’t that be the goal?
